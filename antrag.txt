Subject: API Access Request — donations.create & alerts.create Scope Approval

Hi Streamlabs team,

My name is Julian Mühlhoff and I'm building GroupPool — a web application
that enables multiple stream viewers to pool their donations together and
send a combined tip to a streamer as a group.

--- What GroupPool does ---

Instead of a single viewer donating $1, GroupPool lets 5, 10, or 20 viewers
each contribute a small amount. Once the group reaches their shared goal,
GroupPool sends a single combined donation on their behalf — including a
message that shows the group's names and total contribution.

Example: "5 viewers pooled together to donate €10.00 — GroupPool"

This creates a new kind of community interaction on stream and makes
donating accessible for viewers who wouldn't donate alone.

--- Why we need the Streamlabs API ---

We want to integrate with Streamlabs because it is the most widely used
donation and alert platform among streamers on Twitch and YouTube.

Specifically, we need access to two scopes:

1. donations.create — to submit the pooled donation to the streamer's
   Streamlabs tip page once the group goal is reached

2. alerts.create — to trigger a custom on-screen alert that shows the
   group donation with all participants' names, so the streamer and chat
   can celebrate the moment together

--- Our use case summary ---

- We act on behalf of streamers who connect their Streamlabs account
  to GroupPool via OAuth
- Donations are only triggered when the group goal is reached and all
  participants have confirmed their contribution
- We do not store Streamlabs credentials; we use short-lived OAuth tokens
- Volume is low (group donations are a social event, not automated bulk
  transactions)

--- Current status ---

GroupPool is in active development. Our OAuth app is already registered
in the Streamlabs developer portal. We are currently blocked by the
